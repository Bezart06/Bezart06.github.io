For the Spanish and Turkish markets, the focus is on varieties suitable for both single and cluster harvesting. We aim for round-shaped fruits with strong peel integrity and vibrant color. Our selections incorporate resistances to key threats such as Citrus Tristeza Virus (CTV), Alternaria brown spot, nematodes, and root-related fungal diseases. Some advanced hybrids may also include added resistance to fungal pathogens like Cladosporium and Leveillula taurica.

For Brazilian, Moroccan, and Turkish markets, the breeding emphasizes large round fruits (200 grams and above), ideal for both fresh consumption and export. Disease resistance remains a cornerstone, with current lines integrating CTV, Alternaria, nematode resistance, and rootstock-compatible tolerance.

In the Mediterranean region, our breeding focuses on both round and slightly elongated fruits, spanning from standard sizes (140–180 grams) to larger, premium beef-type selections (180–300 grams and above). All hybrids in this segment are bred with essential disease resistances, including CTV, Alternaria, and nematodes.

Agridera aspires to become a global leader in the breeding, production, and marketing of tangerine seeds. Through intensive investment in research and development, we strive to deliver innovative solutions for growers and consumers across diverse international markets. Our core focus is on developing improved, resilient cultivars tailored to the specific needs of each region.

Our current breeding efforts emphasize integrating resistance to major tangerine diseases, such as Citrus Tristeza Virus (CTV), Huanglongbing (HLB/Greening Disease), and Alternaria brown spot, into elite breeding lines and hybrids. Simultaneously, we are advancing the development of varieties with tolerance to environmental stresses and emerging pests, ensuring durable field performance and crop security.

In terms of horticultural characteristics, our breeding programs target early maturity, various fruit sizes, intense orange peel and flesh color, exceptional flavor, peelability, shelf life, and resistance to fruit splitting. We aim to combine superior fruit quality with robust disease resistance across a range of tangerine types, including easy-peel, seedless, and extended season varieties.

Agridera’s tangerine breeding programs are strategically focused on three key segments: Protected Cultivation Varieties, Open-field High-density Orchards, and Traditional Extensive Orchard Systems. Each segment is tailored to meet the specific agronomic, climatic, and market needs of growers around the world.

Agridera operates through international and local distributers around the world.
